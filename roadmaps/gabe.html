The Gabe Miller Roadmap
Ninja-360 × Lucid Cinematics

Tim Petet × Gabriel Miller. Four buckets, one direction: build the most visible creative operation in Kansas City — and get paid by the work it wins. Each bucket has its own money structure; nothing blurs.

Prepared 2026-07-13 · Companion to the KPI Gameplan ("On the M.A.P.") · Rates set together in writing — blanks filled in the room

01 · Ground Rules

  • Four separate buckets, four separate money structures. Client shoots · the series/Content Engine · the Creative Director role · the Ninja Trainings product line. Each priced on its own terms.
  • Paid when the money exists. Client work pays when the client pays. Series work pays per episode on publish. Courses pay from course revenue. No retainers until recurring revenue supports one.
  • Everything in writing. Rates, splits, IP ownership, credits — one page per bucket, signed by both, versioned.

02 · The Four Buckets

B1 · Client Builds — Premium CaptureNow · every belt close

Scope

Gabe is the premium production tier on Brown and Black Belt builds — cinematic capture, direction, and edit. His fee is a priced line item inside every belt build; Brown ($3,000) and Black ($10,000) were priced specifically to carry this level of production.

Rates (set together, in writing)

  • Day rate: $ · Half-day: $ · Per-edit: $
  • First engagement: the LiveSmart media shoot — fee sized to that deal's cash or paid at go-live.
Structure: per-project, paid when the client pays. Every time. Income scales with closes.
B2 · The Series + Content Engine90-day sprint · E0–E6

Scope

  • Director / DP / editor on "On the M.A.P." — the filmed 12-month KPI campaign (7 episodes in the 90-day sprint, biweekly).
  • The training trade, starting now: Tim teaches 360 editing + mobile DJI workflow → Gabe teaches storytelling, edit craft, and builds the color-grade preset + template library.
  • Success upside: when a client signs traceably from content Gabe directed, he gets a defined cut or the capture work on that build.
Structure: per-episode rate paid on publish — $/episode — plus the training trade (no cash) plus the content-sourced deal cut: %. No retainer.
B3 · Creative Director / Lead EditorIn writing after one paid project

Scope

The role is real and intended for Gabe: co-designs the Stage-4 creative layer (templates, color grade, story structure), owns the creative spec the editing pipeline runs on. It goes in writing after the first paid project together — likely LiveSmart — and includes the one-role vs. two-tier decision (Gabe direction + a volume editor) once Hasan's availability is confirmed.

Path: first paid project delivered → role + authority + comp terms on one page → signed by Friday of that week.
B4 · Ninja Trainings — The Course LineQ4 2026 · after the 90-day sprint

Scope

  • FAA Part 107 prep course — exam preparation for commercial drone certification, built on Tim's Part 107 experience and field practice. (Prep course — the FAA administers the actual test.)
  • 360 photography course — capture, stitch, publish: the Ninja-360 method from Theta/Matterport to Google Street View, taught from real field SOPs.
  • Further NINJA trainings — mobile DJI products, editing craft, visibility fundamentals for owners. Curriculum grows from what the two of you already trade-teach each other.
  • Division of labor: Tim = subject matter + field credibility · Gabe = instructional filming, story structure, edit, and course production quality.

Why this bucket matters

Courses turn expertise into a product that sells while you sleep (the Product pillar), train the future Ninja-360 field techs (the hiring pipeline), and serve the mission on record since day one: teaching young adults drone, 360, and the digital trades. The series audience built in Q1–Q3 becomes the course's launch audience in Q4.

Timing: concept + outline in Q4 planning (October) · production after the day-90 re-score · launch target: Q1 2027 alongside the KC Visibility Report.
Structure: revenue share on course sales — % to Gabe — with IP ownership, credits, and platform terms defined in writing before production starts. No cash up front; the courses pay their makers.
